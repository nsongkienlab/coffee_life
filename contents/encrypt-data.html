Data Encryption

Protecting Sensitive Data

Data encryption is the process of converting plain text into encoded text that only authorized parties can read. This is essential for protecting sensitive data, such as personal information or financial data, from unauthorized access.

Data-at-Rest Encryption

Data-at-rest encryption is the encryption of data that is stored on a device, such as a hard drive or a USB drive. This prevents unauthorized access to the data if the device is lost or stolen.

Data-in-Transit Encryption

Data-in-transit encryption is the encryption of data that is being transmitted over a network, such as the internet or a private network. This prevents unauthorized parties from intercepting and reading the data while it is being transmitted.

End-to-End Encryption

End-to-end encryption is a form of data-in-transit encryption that provides complete security for data transmission. With end-to-end encryption, the data is encrypted at the sender's device and remains encrypted until it is decrypted at the recipient's device, preventing unauthorized access at any point in the transmission process.
