Encryption Algorithms

Symmetric Encryption

Symmetric encryption uses a single key to both encrypt and decrypt data. This key must be kept secret to maintain the security of the encryption. Symmetric encryption is fast and efficient, but can be vulnerable to attacks if the key is compromised.

Asymmetric Encryption

Asymmetric encryption, also known as public-key encryption, uses a pair of keys, one for encryption and one for decryption. The public key can be freely shared, while the private key must be kept secret. Asymmetric encryption is slower than symmetric encryption but provides a higher level of security.

Hashing

Hashing is a form of encryption that converts data of any size into a fixed-size hash value. Hashing is often used for password storage, where the hash value is stored instead of the actual password. When a user logs in, their password is hashed and compared to the stored hash value.

Quantum Encryption

Quantum encryption is a cutting-edge form of encryption that uses quantum mechanics to encrypt and decrypt data. Quantum encryption is extremely secure and is believed to be immune to attacks from even the most powerful computers.
