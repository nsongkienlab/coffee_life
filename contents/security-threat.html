Security Threats

Malware

Malware is any type of malicious software that can infect devices and steal data, disrupt operations, or hold data hostage. Types of malware include viruses, trojans, worms, and ransomware. To prevent malware infections, organizations should use antivirus software, keep software up to date, and train employees to recognize and avoid phishing attacks.

Phishing

Phishing is a type of social engineering attack where attackers use email, text messages, or other means to trick users into disclosing sensitive information. Organizations should implement security measures such as spam filters, two-factor authentication, and employee training to prevent phishing attacks.

Insider Threats

Insider threats can result from malicious or unintentional actions by employees or authorized individuals. Organizations should implement access control measures, conduct background checks on employees, and monitor employee activities to prevent insider threats.

Physical Security Breaches

Physical security breaches occur when unauthorized individuals gain access to physical assets such as servers, data centers, or offices and steal or damage equipment or data. To prevent physical security breaches, organizations should implement physical security measures such as access controls, security cameras, and alarm systems.
